## Construction Grids & Geometry

- `[PENDING REVIEW]` Marks are built on an explicit **construction grid** (geometric guides — circles, squares, golden-ratio/√2 proportions, consistent angle increments) so the form is reproducible and intentional rather than eyeballed.
- `[PENDING REVIEW]` The grid is a *scaffold, not a cage* — the senior move is knowing when to break the geometry for the eye (see optical correction).

## Optical Correction (the senior "why")

- `[PENDING REVIEW]` **Mathematically equal shapes do not look equal.** The eye, not the ruler, is the final judge. Compensations a senior applies:
  - **Overshoot:** circles and triangles must extend slightly past the cap-height/baseline of square letters to *look* the same size (a true-height O reads small).
  - **Horizontal bars** (the middle of E/F/H) sit slightly **above** true center, or they look like they're sliding down.
  - **Pointed/curved terminals** (V, A, W, O) overshoot guides more than flat ones.
  - **Vertical strokes** are drawn slightly heavier than horizontals to read as equal weight.
  - **Junctions/joins** are thinned where strokes meet to avoid dark "blobs."
- `[PENDING REVIEW]` Rule of thumb: *if it measures right but looks wrong, the measurement is wrong.*

## Wordmark Alignment

- `[PENDING REVIEW]` Align on **optical** edges, not bounding boxes; round letters and pointed letters overshoot the baseline/cap line.
- `[PENDING REVIEW]` Tune **x-height, spacing/tracking, and counters** for the mark at its intended sizes — wordmark spacing is set tighter than body text and optically kerned letter-pair by letter-pair.

## Clear-Space / Exclusion Zone

- `[PENDING REVIEW]` Define a minimum clear space as a **multiple of a feature of the mark itself** (a common convention: the cap-height, the x-height, or the width of a key glyph — e.g. "clear space = height of the logotype's cap"). Tying it to the mark keeps it proportional at any scale.

## Minimum Size / Scalability

- `[PENDING REVIEW]` Specify **minimum sizes per medium** (e.g. a px minimum for screen, a mm minimum for print) below which the mark loses legibility — and design so the mark survives to that floor.

## Responsive / Adaptive Logo Systems

- `[PENDING REVIEW]` A modern identity ships a **range of marks**, not one file, degrading gracefully as space/size shrinks:
  - **Full lockup** (mark + wordmark + tagline) → **primary** (mark + wordmark) → **simplified** (wordmark or mark alone) → **monogram / favicon** (initial or symbol).
  - Each step drops detail that won't survive the smaller size while keeping the identity recognizable.
- `[PENDING REVIEW]` Define which variant is used at which size/context as an explicit rule, so downstream surfaces (favicon, app icon, social avatar) don't improvise.

## Accessibility note (verified — see color-and-type-identity-reference)

- The mark should be identifiable in **monochrome** first.
- WCAG **exempts logo/brand-name text** from contrast minimums — don't let a contrast gate false-flag the logo, but do hold functional elements around it to the standard.
