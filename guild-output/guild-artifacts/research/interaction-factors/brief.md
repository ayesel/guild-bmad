# Research brief — Interaction factors — motion, micro-interactions, flow dynamics

**Objective (the decision this informs):** Decide which interaction/time-dimension gates Guild builds next (Rogue/Mage) and which transition/feedback/reversibility fields become required on flow artifacts.

**What we already know (don't re-derive this):**
Guild already ships an 11-principle motion foundation (signature easing family, duration-scales-with-distance, asymmetric enter/exit), motion tokens with a hard gate, a duration envelope, and audit-side checks for duration/easing/reduced-motion presence and loading-indicator honesty. Products STILL feel clunky — principles without per-factor contracts gate nothing. Full specification with per-factor contract follows below.

## Questions to answer
1. What is the complete factor space of superb interaction over time (micro-interaction anatomy, feedback immediacy, undo-over-confirm, direct manipulation feel, choreography/stagger, spatial continuity, transition coverage, interruptibility, focus-through-time, latency choreography, modality parity, form timing, flow momentum, signature-moment budget, sound/haptics) — and what did we miss?
2. For each factor: what primary-source evidence, machine proxy (including proxies that DRIVE the app — event dispatch, frame capture, runtime traces), numeric thresholds, and honest MEASURE/LOOK/ASK tier?
3. Which checks are static, which require driving the app, which are frame-judged LOOK — and what blocks vs advises?
4. What must flow artifacts (state diagrams, user flows) REQUIRE per state pair and interactive element so interaction is designed at flow time, not applied as polish?
5. What is the eyes-only residue only a human watching/feeling the running artifact can verify?
6. Which interaction folklore fails verification, and where do authoritative sources genuinely disagree (durations, gesture thresholds, validation timing, optimistic-UI boundaries)?

## Must cover
- Saffer Microinteractions (trigger/rules/feedback/loops-modes)
- Material motion spec + IBM Carbon motion + Apple HIG Motion/Feedback (note version dates)
- W3C WCAG 2.3.3 and 2.2.2
- NN/g animation and response-time research
- Val Head, Willenskomer UI-animation principles
- peer-reviewed perceived-performance and gesture studies

## Out of scope (do not spend time here)
- static rendered-surface factors (covered)
- re-deriving the 11 motion principles or token-envelope checks
- brand-motion taste (design-direction territory)

## Sourcing
Prefer primary sources and material from the last 18 months; cite every claim.
