# Research brief — GUILD competitive positioning in agent-native design orchestration

**Objective (the decision this informs):** decide GUILD's positioning: is there a direct competitor, and should GUILD compete on generation or on discipline-specialized design orchestration?

**What we already know (don't re-derive this):**
GUILD is an 8-persona design guild (research/IA/interaction/visual/content/QA/ops/design-system) with a hard Sage GO/NO-GO gate, brownfield traceability spine, multi-model raids, and Deep Research expeditions, running natively inside AI coding agents and integrating with BMAD.

## Questions to answer
1. Does any tool orchestrate multiple discipline-specialized DESIGN personas through a full research-to-handoff pipeline?
2. Which tools span the full UX research -> IA -> interaction -> visual -> content -> QA -> handoff lifecycle vs only a slice?
3. Are there open-source repos templated specifically for design workflows inside AI coding agents?
4. Is there a real IDE-native / agent-native design trend, and what form does it take?
5. Who are the closest comparables, and do we compete / complement / integrate with each?
6. Do GUILD's Raid (multi-model) and Expedition (multi-Deep-Research reconcile) mechanics have any first-party equivalent?
7. What are the pricing, business model, and adoption signals of the closest comparables?
